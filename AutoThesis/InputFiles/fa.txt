**Challenges in Model Training**
The code notes significant challenges with LSTM and GRU models, which were initially planned but abandoned due to excessive training times and high computational resource demands. These recurrent neural network architectures, designed for sequential data, struggled with the large dataset of 487,235 records, even when reduced to 200,000 samples. The resource-intensive nature of these models led to RAM crashes, making them impractical for the project’s scope without advanced hardware. This limitation restricted the comparison to four models: DisBERT, FocusCNN, Context RF, and AttentionCNN.

**Why DisBERT Outperformed Other Methods**
DisBERT, a transformer-based model, achieved the highest performance (98.12% accuracy, 1.87% Hamming loss) due to its ability to capture contextual nuances in text through pre-trained embeddings (`distilbert-base-uncased`). Its efficiency in handling smaller datasets (reduced to 10,000 samples due to memory constraints) and fine-tuning with a linear learning rate scheduler (`get_linear_schedule_with_warmup`) optimized performance. In contrast, LSTM and GRU models were computationally infeasible, while AttentionCNN underperformed (94.38% accuracy) due to insufficient tuning. FocusCNN and Context RF, though effective, lacked DisBERT’s contextual depth, making it the most suitable for robust text classification.
