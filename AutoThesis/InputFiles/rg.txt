Many existing studies in AI vs human text detection continue to rely on traditional machine learning models such as Logistic Regression and Random Forest, which lack the ability to capture deeper contextual nuances in text. A key limitation across much of the literature is the absence of effective text cleaning — issues such as unprocessed emojis, contractions, and informal structures often remain untreated. This compromises the model’s ability to distinguish between human and AI-generated content. Additionally, datasets used in many experiments are often imbalanced, leading to biased model training and unreliable evaluation. These gaps—ineffective preprocessing and poor data distribution—limit the generalisability and robustness of detection systems in real-world applications where text is messy and varied.
