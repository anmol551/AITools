This outcome aligns with the assumption that advanced models like TabNet would offer superior prediction accuracy compared to simpler ones such as ElasticNet which only reached an R2 score of 73.88% and accuracy of 82.73%. The key findings suggest that deploying TabNet in loan approval systems could lead to more accurate risk predictions enabling banks to make better decisions on approvals and reduce default rates. For instance the model's accuracy of 89.10% means it can identify high-risk applicants more effectively potentially lowering financial losses for lenders while increasing approval rates for low-risk cases. Benefits include cost savings from fewer bad loans and improved customer satisfaction through fairer processes though risks exist such as the model's sensitivity to data changes which might cause inconsistent results in new environments. In practice this could mean training staff to monitor and update models regularly to avoid errors. Ethical concerns arise from possible biases in data that affect underrepresented groups leading to unfair denials while socially it promotes responsible lending by flagging risks early. From a business view integrating these models into apps like the tested Streamlit application offers streamlined operations but requires investment in resources to handle computational demands ensuring overall system reliability.

5. INTERPRETATION OF RESULTS
----------------------------------------
Discussion on How Findings Answer the Research Question  
The study examined whether models could predict financial risk scores and derive loan approvals accurately from a synthetic dataset. Results show TabNet model delivered the highest regression accuracy with a mean absolute error of 1.90 and an R² score of 0.88 indicating strong alignment between predicted and actual risk scores. This confirms the models effectively address the question by providing reliable risk predictions that support binary decisions on loan approvals.

Direct correlation between results and research objectives  
The research aimed to predict continuous risk scores and convert them to binary classifications. TabNet results directly meet these goals with an accuracy of 89.10% and ROC-AUC of 95.98% reflecting precise score predictions and effective threshold application. Engineered features like Debt Stress Index contributed to this by improving model performance in line with the objective of enhancing prediction accuracy.

Addressing any unexpected findings and their relevance  
While TabNet outperformed expectations with lower false negatives than other models such as CNN the improvement in recall for denied applicants was notable. This suggests potential influences from feature engineering that enhanced detection of high-risk cases. Such findings emphasise the importance of tailored features in refining risk assessment for real-world loan decisions.

6. RESEARCH NOVELTY
----------------------------------------
This research presents several key innovations in financial risk modelling. It introduces a unified approach that combines continuous risk score prediction and binary loan approval decisions into one predictive system. This method uses a single TabNet regressor where the output is thresholded at 50% to generate decisions ensuring consistency between risk assessment and approval outcomes.

The study employs TabNet's attention mechanism with entmax sparse activation and five sequential steps to offer feature-level interpretability. This design suits regulated financial contexts by providing transparency that standard neural networks lack. It also incorporates a virtual batch size of 128 within larger batches of 512 to enhance generalisation on tabular data through ghost batch normalisation avoiding common training instabilities.

Another contribution involves creating six composite financial indicators such as the Debt Stress Index calculated as DebtToIncomeRatio times InterestRate and the Composite Risk Index as a weighted mix of credit score deficit debt ratio interest rate and risk flags. These indicators integrate domain knowledge into the feature set reducing the model's reliance on raw data.

The system adds a governance layer that applies lending policy rules as overrides on predictions above 50%. This two-tier setup combines model inference with rule-based enforcement to align with operational needs.

Finally the integration of GPT-4o-mini transforms numerical outputs into structured six-section underwriting reports. This feature creates natural language justifications that aid audits reviews and communication fostering human-AI teamwork in decision processes.

These elements distinguish the study by advancing model efficiency interpretability and practical application in lending. (298 words)
