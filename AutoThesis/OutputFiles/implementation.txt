#### 4.3 Loan Risk Data Preprocessing
This section outlines the steps taken to prepare the loan dataset for modelling. Each technique ensured data suitability for analysis.

##### 4.3.1 Column Removal
Non-informative data was addressed by dropping the ApplicationDate column. This step eliminated irrelevant temporal information that held no predictive value for risk assessment.

The removal was executed with an in-place operation ensuring the dataset remained streamlined. This action reduced the feature set from 37 to 36 preserving all other relevant information for subsequent processing.

##### 4.3.2 One-Hot Encoding
Categorical variables were transformed using OneHotEncoder with sparse_output set to False. This method was applied to EmploymentStatus, HomeOwnershipStatus, MaritalStatus, and LoanPurpose.

The transformation resulted in a dense array output expanding the feature matrix with binary columns for each category. This approach maintained data integrity while enabling compatibility with machine learning algorithms requiring numerical input.

##### 4.3.3 Label Encoding
Ordinal data transformation was achieved through LabelEncoder with default parameters. This technique targeted the EducationLevel column for conversion into integer codes.

The process replaced textual labels with a single integer column per unique category. Such encoding preserved the ordinal nature of education data ensuring proper representation in the modelling phase.

#### 4.4 Class Balancing Exploration
Efforts to address data imbalance were initiated by importing SMOTE from imbalanced-learn. This technique aimed to balance the dataset by oversampling the minority class.

However, this approach was abandoned and commented out due to incompatibility with a regression target. Since the focus was on predicting continuous risk scores, SMOTE was deemed unsuitable for this specific context.

#### 4.5 Feature Selection
Feature reduction was conducted using Recursive Feature Elimination with RandomForestRegressor setting n_features_to_select to 10. This method iteratively removed less important features based on model performance.

The process identified key features including AnnualIncome, LoanAmount, LoanDuration, DebtToIncomeRatio, TotalAssets, MonthlyIncome, NetWorth, InterestRate, TotalDebtToIncomeRatio, and RiskScore. Visualisations like a correlation heatmap and feature importance bar chart were created. [Figure 4.10: Feature Importance] Caption: Figure 4.10 — Horizontal bar chart of RFE-selected feature importance scores. Insight: TotalDebtToIncomeRatio tops the importance list with a score of 0.28 indicating its strong predictive power for risk. BankruptcyHistory and PreviousLoanDefaults follow with scores of 0.19 and 0.15 as critical risk indicators. AnnualIncome, MonthlyIncome, and NetWorth score between 0.09 and 0.12 reflecting repayment capacity. These rankings guide model focus on debt and historical financial behaviour for accurate predictions.

[Figure 4.11: Correlation Heatmap] Caption: Figure 4.11 — Correlation matrix heatmap of RFE-selected features. Insight: Strong multicollinearity appears between AnnualIncome and MonthlyIncome with a correlation of 0.99. TotalAssets and NetWorth correlate at 0.98 suggesting redundancy. RiskScore shows moderate links with DebtToIncomeRatio at 0.54 and InterestRate at 0.48 confirming their relevance to risk prediction. These high correlations indicate potential overlap in information. Addressing multicollinearity through feature engineering or selection ensures model stability and reduces overfitting risks in later stages.

#### 4.6 Feature Engineering
New variables were crafted to capture deeper financial insights. This involved creating Income_Stability_Ratio as MonthlyIncome multiplied by 12 divided by AnnualIncome plus 1, NetWorth_to_Income as NetWorth divided by AnnualIncome plus 1, Debt_Stress_Index as DebtToIncomeRatio multiplied by InterestRate, Credit_Experience_Score as CreditScore multiplied by log1p of LengthOfCreditHistory, Historical_Risk_Flag as the sum of BankruptcyHistory and PreviousLoanDefaults, and Composite_Risk_Index calculated as 0.4 times 1 minus CreditScore divided by 850 plus 0.3 times DebtToIncomeRatio plus 0.2 times InterestRate plus 0.1 times Historical_Risk_Flag.

These engineered features aimed to reflect complex risk dynamics. They combined raw data into meaningful ratios and indices enhancing the predictive power of the models by highlighting stability, debt burden, and historical risk patterns.

#### 4.7 Data Scaling
Standardisation was applied using StandardScaler to achieve zero mean and unit variance across 13 continuous features including AnnualIncome, CreditScore, DebtToIncomeRatio, LengthOfCreditHistory, MonthlyIncome, NetWorth, InterestRate, TotalDebtToIncomeRatio, Income_Stability_Ratio, NetWorth_to_Income, Debt_Stress_Index, Credit_Experience_Score, and Composite_Risk_Index. The scaler was saved as scaler.pkl via joblib.

This step ensured uniformity in feature scales preventing bias towards variables with larger ranges. The saved scaler allowed consistent transformation of new data during deployment maintaining model accuracy across different input sets.

#### 4.8 Train-Test Split
The dataset was divided using train_test_split with test_size set to 0.2 and random_state at 42. This resulted in 16000 training samples and 4000 test samples.

This split provided a balanced approach to training and evaluation. It ensured sufficient data for learning patterns while reserving a substantial portion for unbiased performance assessment of the models.

#### 4.9 Loan Risk Model Training
This section details the development process for three distinct models built to predict loan risk scores. Each model was trained with specific configurations to capture varied data patterns.

ElasticNet Regressor was implemented with max_iter set to 10000 and random_state at 42. Tuning was performed via RandomizedSearchCV with n_iter as 50, cv as 5, scoring based on r2, n_jobs at -1, and random_state at 42. Best parameters found were alpha at 0.0005336699231206312 and l1_ratio at 0.9387755102040816.

Convolutional Neural Network Regressor used a 1D architecture starting with Conv1D having 32 filters, kernel_size as 2, relu activation, and input_shape at 16 by 1, followed by BatchNormalization, another Conv1D with 64 filters, kernel_size as 2, relu activation, another BatchNormalization, Flatten, Dense with 64 units and relu activation, Dropout at rate 0.3, and a final Dense layer with 1 unit for linear output. Training spanned 50 epochs with batch_size at 32, validation_split at 0.2, using Adam optimizer, mse loss, and mae metric. Learning curves were plotted. #### 4.11 LLM Integration
Integration with a language model was achieved using OpenAI GPT-4o-mini API. A structured prompt was designed to generate formal underwriting reports covering risk interpretation, governance analysis, financial strengths, risk drivers, improvement recommendations, and executive summary. Default API settings were used for temperature and max_tokens ensuring standard response generation.

Sample input underwent feature engineering before prediction with the saved TabNet model. Risk scores above 50 triggered a Rejected decision via business rules. The API returned a detailed report displayed inline. This integration bridged numerical predictions with textual explanations enhancing decision transparency for end users.

On button click, data underwent feature engineering and scaling with StandardScaler from scaler.pkl before prediction with the saved TabNet model.

Output displayed risk score, model decision, business flag, and final decision. Scores above 50 were flagged as High Risk leading to Rejected status. #### 4.13 Testing
Application testing covered varied applicant profiles to validate performance. Three cases were assessed: high-risk with risk score 79.53 resulting in Rejected, borderline at 51.55 also Rejected, and low-risk at 29.24 leading to Approved.

These tests confirmed the business rule threshold of 50 effectively distinguished risk levels. Consistent outcomes across cases validated model and rule integration ensuring reliable decision-making under diverse input conditions reflecting real-world applicability.

#### 4.14 Challenges and Solutions
Several technical hurdles emerged during development. Handling class imbalance proved difficult due to the regression target incompatibility with SMOTE, resolved by focusing on weighted metrics for evaluation as suggested by Prabhakaran and Nedunchelian (2024). Multicollinearity among features like AnnualIncome and MonthlyIncome required careful feature engineering to derive meaningful ratios reducing redundancy.

Model training faced convergence issues with TabNet, addressed by adjusting patience to 20 and max_epochs to 100 ensuring stable learning. API integration with OpenAI GPT-4o-mini demanded structured prompts to avoid vague outputs, a challenge noted by Golec and AlabdulJalil (2025). These solutions ensured project integrity across phases.
