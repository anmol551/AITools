1. CONCLUSION
----------------------------------------
The research focused on developing a machine learning pipeline to distinguish between AI-generated and human-generated text with a dataset of over 487,000 records, split into 62.8% human and 37.2% AI content. The pipeline integrated various models, with DistilBERT standing out by achieving an impressive accuracy of 98.12%, alongside precision, recall, and F1 scores all around 98.12%. Other approaches like FocusCNN reached 97.70% accuracy, Context RF attained 97.05%, and AttentionCNN recorded 94.38%. This pipeline followed a detailed process of data loading, cleaning text through steps like removing punctuation, and creating features such as text length or word count. Training involved a single epoch with a small batch size and a specific learning rate, ensuring effective results despite resource limits that restricted the sample size to 10,000 for DistilBERT.

A fresh aspect of this work lies in combining deep learning with context-aware methods through a hybrid structure. The integration of a three-stage text cleaning process, covering basic, advanced, and linguistic adjustments, helped refine input quality for better classification. This approach, paired with tools like SentenceTransformer for embeddings, brought a new angle to identifying text origins by focusing on semantic depth and reducing noise in the data.

Compared to the baseline study, this research showed clear progress with higher accuracy rates, particularly with DistilBERT surpassing earlier benchmarks. The structured preprocessing and choice of efficient models contributed to this edge, making the system more reliable for detecting differences in text sources.

The web application aspect added practical value by offering an easy-to-use interface built with Dash and styled for clarity. It used a lightweight Random Forest model with 97.05% accuracy, ensuring quick responses through streamlined embedding processes and displaying results with confidence visuals for better user understanding. This blend of technical strength and accessible design marked a step forward in applying such tools in real settings, balancing performance with usability across diverse contexts. The comprehensive analysis of text patterns through visual tools also provided deeper insights into distinct traits of human and AI writing, aiding the refinement of classification techniques. Together, these elements formed a cohesive framework that advanced the capability to discern text origins with precision and practicality for broader implementation.

2. FUTURE WORK
----------------------------------------
The exploration of AI versus human text detection offers several pathways for future research and methodological refinement. Building on the current study, which achieved high accuracy with DisBERT at 98.12%, there is scope to refine the approach by testing alternative machine learning frameworks or deeper neural architectures. Models such as BERT or RoBERTa could capture more complex linguistic patterns compared to DistilBERT. Integrating diverse data sources, like social media snippets or academic writings, may improve predictive strength by broadening the textual variety. Refining feature selection through automated methods or domain-specific criteria could enhance both interpretability and precision. Adopting advanced evaluation metrics, such as cross-validation across varied datasets or error analysis, would ensure more reliable validation of model performance.

Expanding the research, a deeper look into preprocessing strategies is vital. Different text cleaning methods or tokenisation approaches might alter model outcomes significantly. Testing the impact of removing or retaining certain stopwords could reveal hidden influences on accuracy. Applying the findings to practical settings, such as content moderation platforms or educational tools, would bridge the gap between theory and real-world use. Industries dealing with automated content could benefit from tailored detection systems. Ethical dimensions also deserve attention, particularly fairness in predictions. Models must be scrutinised to avoid bias against specific writing styles or demographics, ensuring equitable application across varied contexts.

Further investigation could probe hybrid architectures combining transformer models with traditional classifiers to balance efficiency and depth. Exploring multilingual datasets would test the generalisation of detection mechanisms beyond English texts. Addressing computational constraints by optimising model training or using cloud resources might allow larger datasets to be processed, as seen in the current limitation of 487,235 records. Examining temporal trends in AI-generated content could uncover evolving patterns, aiding in adaptive detection systems. Collaborative efforts with linguists might refine feature engineering by identifying subtle stylistic markers. These directions aim to strengthen the foundation laid by this study, pushing towards more comprehensive and fair text detection solutions.
