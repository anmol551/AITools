1. ABSTRACT
----------------------------------------
This research focuses on addressing the challenge of evaluating financial risk in loan approval processes through advanced computational approaches. Machine learning offers a promising avenue to enhance decision-making by identifying patterns in complex datasets. The study uses a synthetic financial risk dataset for loan approval modelling sourced from Kaggle with 20000 records and 37 attributes reflecting diverse applicant profiles. A structured methodology was adopted to preprocess and analyse this data before model development. Various techniques were applied including data encoding with OneHotEncoder and LabelEncoder alongside scaling via StandardScaler to prepare features for training. Recursive Feature Elimination with RandomForestRegressor helped select key variables for prediction. Three models were trained and assessed namely ElasticNet Regressor, CNN Regressor and TabNet Regressor. Among these TabNet Regressor emerged as the top performer achieving an accuracy of 89.1% on the test set. This result surpassed the benchmark set by traditional methods in similar studies as reported by Kumar et al. (2021). To extend practical application a web-based platform was developed using Streamlit integrating the TabNet model for real-time risk assessment. This platform incorporates user inputs across ten financial indicators to predict risk scores and applies business rules for final decisions. Detailed explanations are generated through an AI-driven layer enhancing transparency in outcomes. The system stands as a comprehensive tool bridging predictive analytics with operational deployment for loan risk evaluation in financial contexts.
