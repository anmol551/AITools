1. ABSTRACT
----------------------------------------
The challenge of distinguishing between AI-generated and human-written text has grown with advancements in language models creating content that mimics human writing. Machine learning offers a promising approach to tackle this issue by identifying subtle patterns in text structure and style. This research focuses on the AI vs. Human Text dataset comprising around 500,000 essays sourced from various platforms with duplicates removed to ensure data quality. A systematic methodology was adopted to preprocess the data and build classification models capable of detecting the origin of text. Techniques such as text cleaning tokenisation and feature extraction were applied alongside models like FocusCNN AttentionCNN Context RF and DisBERT. Among these DisBERT emerged as the top performer achieving an accuracy of 98.12% surpassing established benchmarks in this domain. A web application was developed using the Dash framework to provide an interactive platform for users to input text and receive predictions on whether it is AI-generated or human-written. This application integrates the trained model to deliver results with confidence scores and visual indicators for better user understanding. The superior performance of DisBERT highlights the potential of transformer-based architectures in capturing contextual differences between AI and human text. This study contributes to the growing field of text origin detection by offering a practical solution through both model accuracy and an accessible interface for real-world application. The balance of technical depth and usability ensures that the outcomes are valuable for academic exploration and practical deployment in identifying text authenticity across varied contexts.
